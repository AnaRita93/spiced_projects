<!-- song facts -->
<div class="panel album-panel noprint">
Florence + The Machine is a fan of the TV series. It turned out she is the last performer to contribute a song to the show. She said about the music written by Ramin Djawadi, <i>"The notes of sounded like a Celtic folk song to me. I thought it was really beautiful. I love the idea of dancing with ghosts and never wanting to leave. That totally makes sense to me. I feel like I do that every night on stage."</i>
</div>
<div class="panel album-panel noprint">
The creators of the "Game of Thrones" expressed their feeling about having Florence + The Machine's song, <i>"We've always been huge fans of Florence's music. So the opportunity to hear her otherworldly voice on our show was always at the forefront of our minds. We're still pleasantly shocked that she agreed to sing 'Jenny of Oldstones', and we're in love with the result."</i>
</div>
<div class="panel album-panel noprint">
"Jenny Of Oldstones" is heard in the second episode of the series' eighth season, "A Knight of the Seven Kingdoms". Character Podrick Payne performs it in the episode. Florence + The Machine's version is played during the end credits.
</div>
